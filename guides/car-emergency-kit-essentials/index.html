FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about emergency preparedness and how our resource can help you stay safe.

Every emergency kit should include water (one gallon per person per day for at least three days), non-perishable food, a flashlight, extra batteries, a first aid kit, a whistle, dust masks, personal sanitation supplies, a wrench or pliers, local maps, and a portable phone charger. The specific items may vary depending on your situation, which is why we provide customized checklists for different emergency scenarios.
Most emergency supplies should be reviewed and updated every six months. Water should be replaced every six months, while food items should be checked for expiration dates annually. Batteries should be tested and replaced as needed, medications should be refreshed before they expire, and seasonal items like warm blankets or sun protection should be rotated based on the time of year. We recommend setting calendar reminders twice a year to review your kit.
